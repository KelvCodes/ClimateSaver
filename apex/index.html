DIY Composting Guide

Turn your food scraps into nutrient-rich soil with these simple steps.

1

Choose Your Compost Bin

You can use a store-bought bin or make your own from wood pallets or plastic containers.

2

Balance Green & Brown Materials

Greens (Nitrogen-rich): Fruit/veggie scraps, coffee grounds, fresh grass clippings
Browns (Carbon-rich): Dry leaves, cardboard, newspaper, wood chips

3

Layer Your Materials

Start with browns, add greens, and keep a 3:1 ratio (browns to greens).

4

Maintain Your Pile

Turn the pile every 1-2 weeks to aerate. It should feel like a damp sponge.

5

Harvest Your Compost

In 2-6 months, you'll have dark, crumbly compost ready for your garden!

What You Can Compost

DO Compost

  • Fruit & vegetable scraps
  • Eggshells
  • Coffee grounds & filters
  • Tea bags
  • Yard trimmings

DON'T Compost

  • Meat or dairy
  • Fats/oils
  • Diseased plants
  • Pet waste
  • Coal/charcoal
